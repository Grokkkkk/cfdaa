STUDENT FUND GRANT



The Catalog of Federal Domestic Assistance (CFDA) offers a variety of financial aid options aimed at reducing the need for student loans and supporting debt-free education. Key offerings include federal, state, institutional, and private grants tailored to meet different financial needs and educational goals.





Student loan resolvement

The average federal student loan debt is $37850. In total, about 43 million Americans share $1.6 trillion in federal student loan we offer a debt resolve approach for students negotiating on behalf of the individual from a federal approach resulting to almost absolute zero payback policy.

Tuition assistance programs

Our ultimate goal is to help students past or present to achieve financial stability by significantly reducing or even eliminating their student loan debt through a combination of tuition assistance federal programs and tailored negotiation strategies. By leveraging these existing federal mechanisms and our negotiation expertise, we aim to provide comprehensive support that results in almost absolute zero payback for students.

Scholarship Fund Grant

The CFDA scholarship fund grant is a financial award given to students based on various criteria such as academic merit, financial need, community service, or specific talents. Unlike loans, scholarships do not need to be repaid, making them a valuable resource for students seeking to reduce the cost of their education and avoid debt. We provide these grants in collaboration with educational institutions, private organizations, nonprofits, and government agencies to support students in pursuing their academic and career goals.
